Tribute to Shane Warne

Shane Warne

Australia's Legendary Legspinner

Here's a glimpse of his International Career:

  • 1969 : Born in Victoria, Australia
  • 1992–1993 : He had an undistinguished Test debut when called into the Australian team in January 1992 for a Test against India at the Sydney Cricket Ground.
  • 1993–1999 : Warne was to be a key member of Australia's squad for the 1996 World Cup, held in India, Pakistan and Sri Lanka. Australia qualified for the final, with Warne having taken 12 wickets, including a man-of-the-match 4/36 in the semi-final against the West Indies.
  • 1999–2000 : After his World Cup performances, Warne was retained as Australia's vice-captain for the tours of Sri Lanka and Zimbabwe later in 1999.
  • 2001–2003 : Australia played a three-Test series against Pakistan in October 2002, held in neutral Sri Lanka and the United Arab Emirates. Warne, who had lost weight over the previous months, took 27 wickets, was named the player of the series, and was man of the match in the first Test (with 11 wickets) and the third Test (with eight wickets).

  • 2004–2006 : Warne returned to competitive cricket following his ban in February 2004.
  • 2006–2007 : WWarne began his 2006/07 Ashes campaign with an indifferent Test in Brisbane and a poor first innings showing in Adelaide.
  • 2008–2013 : After his retirement from international cricket, Warne was signed as the captain for Rajasthan Royals in the Indian Premier League 2008, fetching US$450,000 in the pre-season player auction.
  • 2022 : Warne died in his villa in Koh Samui, Thailand on 4 March 2022 due to a suspected heart attack.
